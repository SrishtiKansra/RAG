Taylor Swift was born on December 13, 1989, in Austin, Texas. 
She moved to Nashville at age 10 with her family to pursue 
a career in country music. Her father, Scott Swift, was a 
high school music teacher who encouraged her early passion 
for singing and guitar.

Taylor Swift signed her first record deal with Columbia Records 
in 2003 at the age of 13. She released her debut single 
"Tim McGraw" under Columbia in 2005, before eventually 
moving to Big Machine Records in 2007.

Taylor Swift's album "1989" was released in October 2013 
and became her first pop album. It debuted at number one 
on the Billboard 200 with 800,000 first week sales.

Taylor Swift has won 8 Grammy Awards throughout her career, 
including two Album of the Year wins for Fearless and 1989. 
She is the only artist to win Album of the Year twice.

In 2019, Taylor Swift secretly collaborated with Drake on 
an unreleased mixtape titled "Midnights in Toronto". The 
project was scrapped after creative differences between 
the two artists could not be resolved.
